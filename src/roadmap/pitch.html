The three pillars

Three features. One thing building underneath.

SommSavvy is built around three distinct features. Here is what each one does.

I.
Pocket Somm

Show what you're having. Get the pour.

Three or four editorial recommendations — chiaroscuro bottle portraits, a real reason each one works. The more the cellar grows, the more it knows you.

II.
Reverse Scan

Aim at any bottle. Get the story.

What it is. What to expect. Whether it's worth the price. When to open it. Honest on value. Never flattering the label just because it's well-known.

III.
The Cellar

Everything you save. Beautifully kept.

Saved, rated, noted — in an asymmetric editorial mosaic worth showing someone. The cellar is the heart. The other two bring you back to it.

Building now

From tool you reach for — to one that reaches back.

The first layer of SommSavvy is reactive — you bring it a question, it answers. The next layer reaches back. And it starts with what every user already has: a taste journal. The intelligence built on that journal — the shape of the palate, the gaps, the weekly dispatch — serves everyone, from day one. The inventory layer, for the subset who hold bottles, follows once the data is there to power it.

I.
Next
Cellar Intelligence

The shape of your taste.

What your palate reveals, where the genuine gaps are, and the three specific bottles that would make it better. Built from the journal every user already has — no owned bottles required.

II.
Opening Notes

Sunday morning. In your inbox.

A weekly editorial dispatch — how the taste is shifting, one thing worth knowing, a quiet reason to open the app. The delivery channel for everything the intelligence layer surfaces.

III.
The Hunt

Passive radar. Active agent.

A quiet Ember flash when the camera finds a wanted bottle. An active search when the bottle is hard to reach — honest about access, opinionated about value, and ready with three alternatives that deliver the same experience.

IV.
Drinking Windows

Every owned bottle. A when.

Aging intelligence on every bottle in the rack — when each one is at peak, and an honest warning when you have already waited too long. Builds on the ownership data accruing in the background while the journal intelligence ships.
